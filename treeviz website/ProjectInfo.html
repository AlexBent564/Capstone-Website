A User-friendly Platform for Visualizing Tree Growth


Background

Climate change is causing detrimental effects on plants and animals. Trees, which cover nearly half of all land area contribute massively to the exchange of carbon with the atmosphere.

To better understand how trees affect and are affected by climate change, Dr. Kiona Ogle of Ogle labs developed a program that simulates tree growth. The program is called the Allometrically Constrained Growth and Carbon Allocation (ACGCA) model.

Project Description


The problem..

is that the model requires at least some knowledge in programming. This severely limits the range of impact that the program could have in educating people about the relationship between tree growth and climate change.


The solution..

is a user-friendly graphical interface. Team TreeViz will create a web application that will allow users of any background to utilize the ACGCA model. Suddenly, Dr. Ogle's model will be reaching the minds of a much broader audience, helping them better understand how tree growth is affected by climate change.


Solution Requirements:

To organize development of our web application, we have separated it into its basic requirements.

  • An aesthetic and easy-to-naviagate graphical user interface
  • A way for users to enter in any of the 30+ inputs to the model
  • A visually appealing output representation of the simulated tree
  • A way for users to save their inputs for later use
